History of Newcastle United

Newcastle United Football Club was founded in 1892 after the merger of two local teams, Newcastle West End and Newcastle East End. The club quickly became a symbol of pride for the city of Newcastle upon Tyne.

Nicknamed the Magpies for their black-and-white striped kits, Newcastle has a rich tradition of passionate fans and famous players.

Throughout its history, Newcastle United has played at the iconic St James' Park, one of the most famous football grounds in England.

Major Achievements

  • 4-time English First Division Champions (1905, 1907, 1909, 1927)
  • 6-time FA Cup winners (1909, 1932, 1951, 1952, 1955, 1999 finalists)
  • European Inter-Cities Fairs Cup winners (1969)
  • Premier League runners-up (1996, 1997)

Modern Era

Newcastle have seen many highs and lows in modern times, including exciting Champions League nights, promotion battles, and the incredible support of their fans. Today, Newcastle United is focused on building a bright future with a strong squad and ambitious plans.
